## Short Report:
"Echoes in the Storm" presents a compelling rescue narrative with strong central characters and a well-defined conflict that effectively drives the story forward. The prose is clear and the pacing maintains tension throughout, particularly during the climactic confrontation. However, the story would benefit from deeper character development for Eliza, more organic integration of backstory elements, and addressing some logical inconsistencies in the plot, particularly regarding James's secret identity and the resolution.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen Eliza's character arc**: While Eliza is clearly motivated, she doesn't undergo significant personal growth. Consider adding moments that challenge her worldview or force her to confront personal weaknesses, creating a more transformative journey.

* **Strengthen the logic of James's subplot**: The revelation of James as the janitor feels somewhat contrived. Develop this subplot earlier with subtle clues that make his identity reveal feel earned rather than convenient.

* **Add more sensory details**: While the blizzard setting creates tension, the story would benefit from more consistent sensory details that immerse readers in the harsh environment and heighten the danger.

* **Develop the antagonist**: Malcolm's motivations could be more nuanced beyond jealousy and greed. Consider showing moments that humanize him or explain his descent into villainy more fully.

* **Address logistical questions**: Clarify how certain events are possible during a severe blizzard, such as finding the lodge or the timing of various characters' arrivals, to strengthen the story's internal logic.
