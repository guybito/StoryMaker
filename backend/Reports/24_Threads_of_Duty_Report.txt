## Summary Report:
"Threads of Duty" is a compelling historical fiction that effectively explores racial tensions and progressive ideals in the post-Reconstruction South. The story excels in character development, particularly in establishing clear motivations and conflicts, with Elijah and the supporting cast being well-realized within their historical context. The narrative maintains strong tension throughout, with escalating conflicts that build to a satisfying climax and resolution. While the prose is generally strong and the historical setting convincingly rendered, some plot elements could benefit from further development to avoid feeling contrived.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Strengthen the backstory elements**: Provide more specific details about Elijah's earlier life and how he came to his position as foreman to deepen reader connection with his character.
* **Develop the account books subplot more fully**: The financial scheme to fund the mill is introduced but not fully resolved in the ending, creating a loose end that should be addressed.
* **Make Isaiah's arrival feel less coincidental**: Establish earlier foreshadowing or explanation for how Isaiah discovered his son's whereabouts to make his crucial intervention feel more organic to the story.
* **Add more sensory details to key scenes**: While the writing is generally strong, incorporating more sensory elements (sounds, smells, textures) would make pivotal moments like the confrontation scene more immersive.
* **Clarify Bedford's change of heart**: His quick acceptance of the mill project at the end feels somewhat abrupt; showing more of his internal calculation would make this transition more believable.
