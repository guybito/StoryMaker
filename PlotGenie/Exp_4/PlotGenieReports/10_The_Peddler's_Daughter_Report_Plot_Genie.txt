## Analysis Report:
"The Peddler's Daughter" is a well-crafted story that successfully blends elements of Irish folklore with a character-driven narrative about grief, belonging, and redemption. The story's greatest strengths lie in its character development, particularly Donal's emotional journey from isolation to connection, and its effective use of conflict to drive both plot and character growth. The integration of supernatural elements with human drama creates a rich narrative texture that enhances rather than distracts from the core emotional themes.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Character physicality**: While Donal and Maeve are well-described physically, other characters like Fiona could benefit from more consistent physical descriptions to help readers visualize them more clearly.

* **Supernatural elements**: The nature of the Merrow's influence over Finnegan could be more explicitly developed to strengthen the logical consistency of this subplot and clarify the stakes of their arrangement.

* **Setting details**: Though the coastal Irish setting is established effectively, more sensory details about Ballycove itself (sounds, smells, daily life) would further immerse readers in this world and strengthen the sense of what the characters are fighting to preserve.

* **Pacing**: The middle section of the story could be tightened slightly to maintain momentum between the initial treasure hunt and the climactic confrontation with the Merrow.
