## Overall Weighted Score: 3.53

## Summary Report:
"The Midnight Express" is a well-crafted thriller with strong character development and an engaging central conflict. The protagonist's internal struggle with guilt effectively parallels the external threat to the train, creating a satisfying narrative arc. The story excels in its atmospheric setting and clear prose, with vivid descriptions that bring the period train journey to life. While some plot elements could be more thoroughly explained, the overall narrative logic is sound and the character transformations feel earned.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen Viktor's redemption arc**: His transition from villain to self-sacrificing hero, while emotionally powerful, feels somewhat abrupt. Additional scenes showing his internal conflict earlier would make his final choice more believable.

* **Clarify Seraphina's abilities**: The ambiguity around whether she has genuine psychic powers or is simply highly observant creates some confusion. Either approach could work, but the story would benefit from a clearer position.

* **Strengthen the technical plausibility**: Some logistical details, such as how Viktor planted explosives on the bridge and how he knew exactly how to redirect the explosion, strain credibility. A few additional lines explaining his expertise or methods would address this.

* **Develop the sociological layer of character development**: While the physical and psychological aspects of characters are well-established, their social contexts (class, cultural background, etc.) could be more consistently integrated to add depth.
