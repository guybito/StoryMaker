## Summary Report
"Breaking Free" presents a compelling character-driven narrative about caregiver burden and personal liberation. The story excels in character development, particularly in portraying Yael's internal struggles and transformation. The conflict is well-constructed and emotionally resonant, touching on universal themes of duty versus self-fulfillment. The major weakness is the confusing naming convention (the father being called Hannah while the sister shares the same name), which creates unnecessary confusion in an otherwise strong narrative. The resolution feels earned and satisfying, with Yael finding a balanced approach to family obligation and personal fulfillment.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Resolve the naming confusion**: The father should have a different name than the sister to avoid reader confusion. This is particularly important since both characters play significant roles in the story.

* **Strengthen the opening**: Establish Yael's desires and the stakes more clearly from the beginning to help readers connect with her struggle immediately.

* **Add more sensory details**: Incorporate more physical descriptions and sensory elements to make scenes more vivid and immersive, particularly when depicting the father's episodes and Yael's artistic process.

* **Clarify practical aspects of caregiving**: Provide more realistic details about how Yael manages work while caregiving and why professional help wasn't sought earlier to address potential reader questions.

* **Develop supporting characters further**: While the supporting cast serves the story well, characters like Liam and Mary could be more fully developed to add additional perspectives on Yael's situation.
