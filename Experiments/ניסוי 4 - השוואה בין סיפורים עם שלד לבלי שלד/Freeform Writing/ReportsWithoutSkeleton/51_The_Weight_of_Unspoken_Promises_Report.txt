## Summary Report
"The Weight of Unspoken Promises" is a well-crafted emotional journey exploring the tension between honoring family legacy and pursuing personal dreams. The story excels in character development, particularly Claire's internal conflict and transformation. The central conflict is compelling and relatable, touching on universal themes of grief, obligation, and moving forward. The prose is clear and effective, with appropriate descriptive elements enhancing the narrative without overwhelming it. While some plot elements could be further developed, the story succeeds in creating an emotionally satisfying resolution.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Develop Miguel's character more fully**: Explaining his five-year silence would make his delayed revelation more credible and add depth to his character. His guilt alone may not fully justify such prolonged silence, especially if he was close to the family.

* **Add more realistic business negotiation**: The buyers' quick acceptance of Claire's modified proposal feels too convenient. Showing some resistance or negotiation would add tension and realism to the resolution while highlighting Claire's determination.

* **Expand the sensory details in key scenes**: While the story includes effective descriptive elements, adding more immersive sensory details during emotional moments (like Miguel's revelation or Claire's final decision) would strengthen the reader's connection to these pivotal scenes.

* **Provide glimpses of Claire's life beyond the boat**: Brief mentions of her education, friends beyond Tara, or other aspects of her life would round out her character and clarify how the Marine Biology Institute connects to her broader life goals.
