## Summary Report
"The Gift of Letting Go" is a well-crafted emotional journey that effectively explores themes of healing and moving forward after disappointment. The story's greatest strengths lie in its character development, particularly Maya's transformation, and its exploration of universal human experiences of loss and renewal. The writing is technically proficient with clear, evocative prose that creates a strong sense of place. While the conflict is primarily internal, it successfully drives the narrative and creates emotional investment in Maya's journey.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Establish stakes earlier**: Consider introducing the specific consequences of Maya's inability to let go in the opening paragraphs to create immediate tension and investment in her journey.

* **Develop supporting characters**: While Grandma Eliza is well-developed through her letters, characters like Leila could benefit from more distinctive traits or interactions to strengthen their contribution to the narrative.

* **Address minor logical issues**: Explain how the lawyer knew when to send the letter and how the box remained undisturbed, or how Leila happened to be in Minneapolis at that exact time, to strengthen the story's internal logic.

* **Balance internal/external conflict**: Consider introducing more external obstacles or challenges for Maya to overcome, which would complement the well-developed internal conflict and create additional narrative tension.

* **Enhance physical descriptions**: While the setting descriptions are strong, more physical details about the characters would help readers visualize them more completely and connect with them on multiple levels.
