## Short Report
"The Watcher in the Woods" presents a compelling psychological horror narrative with strong character motivation and escalating supernatural tension. The protagonist's inner conflict between career ambition and personal safety creates genuine stakes, while the ambiguous entity serves as both antagonist and dark muse. The writing effectively establishes a foreboding atmosphere through vivid environmental descriptions, though supporting characters are limited. The story successfully blends internal and external conflicts to create a satisfying arc with appropriate ambiguity.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen supporting characters**: Even brief appearances by the property manager or flashbacks with Derek could provide greater contrast to Morgan's isolation and highlight her character traits through interaction rather than exposition.

* **Further develop the entity's mythology**: While ambiguity serves horror well, providing slightly more concrete folklore details would strengthen the worldbuilding and give readers additional context for understanding the supernatural elements.

* **Enhance physical descriptions of Morgan**: While her psychological state is well-rendered, more specific physical descriptions would help readers visualize her transformative journey from anxiety to a kind of dark acceptance.

* **Clarify the relationship between Morgan's writing and the entity**: The connection between her creative process and the entity's manifestations could be more explicitly developed to strengthen the thematic elements about artistic inspiration and its costs.
