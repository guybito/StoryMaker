## Summary Report:
"The Last Exhibit" presents a well-crafted narrative about artistic struggle and redemption with strong character development and thematic resonance. The story effectively explores the transformative power of rejection through Ethan's journey from insecurity to confidence. While the plot structure is sound, some aspects of the resolution come somewhat easily, and certain visual and contextual details could be more fully developed to enhance immersion and believability.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen the struggle**: The resolution through Maya's help comes relatively quickly after the initial rejection. Extending Ethan's period of struggle would heighten the emotional impact of his eventual success and make his transformation more earned.

* **Enhance visual descriptions**: Provide more specific, sensory details about Ethan's art installations to help readers visualize what makes his work distinctive and powerful, rather than relying primarily on conceptual descriptions.

* **Develop Chen's character arc**: Her shift from rejecting Ethan's work to appreciating it would be more convincing with additional scenes showing her perspective evolving, perhaps through observing audience reactions or reconsidering her initial assessment.

* **Expand the financial stakes**: Since Ethan's depleted savings are mentioned early, addressing how he manages financially during the six months between exhibitions would add realism and strengthen the stakes.

* **Incorporate more art world context**: Adding brief details about current trends or competing artists would enrich the setting and better explain why Ethan's work was initially rejected but later appreciated.
