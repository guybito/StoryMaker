## Summary Report:
"The Mortician's Dilemma" is a well-crafted crime story that effectively combines elements of mystery, character development, and psychological tension. The protagonist's journey from social isolation to courageous action forms a compelling character arc that parallels the external murder investigation. The story's strengths lie in its character development, consistent conflict escalation, and clear prose. While some plot elements could be more thoroughly explained and certain scenes could benefit from additional sensory detail, the overall narrative maintains strong coherence and engagement throughout.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen the setting descriptions**: While the morgue atmosphere is established, additional sensory details (sounds, textures, temperature variations) would create an even more immersive environment and heighten the tension during key scenes.

* **Clarify some plot mechanics**: Provide more specific details about how Vanessa accessed the morgue after hours and how exactly she administered the poison to her husband, which would strengthen the logical foundation of the mystery.

* **Develop secondary character backgrounds**: While Eliza is relatively well-developed, other supporting characters like Dr. Patel and Detective Rivera could benefit from brief additional details about their backgrounds to make them more three-dimensional.

* **Expand on the technical aspects of the investigation**: Including more specific forensic details about the poison detection and autopsy findings would enhance the authenticity of the medical examiner setting and increase reader investment in the procedural elements.

* **Consider adding more obstacles during the climactic confrontation**: The resolution of the standoff with Vanessa happens somewhat quickly; additional complications during this sequence would heighten tension and showcase more of Marcus's resourcefulness.
