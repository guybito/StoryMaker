## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Character physicality**: Enhance physical descriptions of some characters, particularly the brothers Erik and Emil, to help readers better distinguish between them despite their identical appearance. This would strengthen the visual dimension of the narrative.

* **Control room logistics**: Clarify how the children gained access to critical station systems and why such systems would be vulnerable to unauthorized control. This would address a potential plot hole that affects the story's credibility.

* **Kessler's research**: Develop the subplot regarding Kessler's biological weapons research more thoroughly earlier in the narrative to make the children's accusations feel more integrated rather than suddenly introduced.

* **Show don't tell**: Replace some instances of directly stating emotional states with physical manifestations or actions that demonstrate those feelings, particularly during key emotional moments like Elias's relief when Kessler accepts his relationship with Linnea.

* **Resolution pacing**: Consider extending the resolution phase to allow for a more gradual healing of Kessler and reconciliation between characters, which would feel more realistic given the severity of the conflicts established.
