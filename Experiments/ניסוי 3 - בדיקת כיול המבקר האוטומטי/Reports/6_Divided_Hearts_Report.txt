## Summary Report:
"Divided Hearts" presents a compelling Romeo and Juliet narrative in a contemporary Native American/White American context, with well-developed characters facing realistic cultural and familial obstacles. The story effectively builds tension through escalating conflicts and maintains thematic consistency throughout its exploration of identity, belonging, and impossible love. The prose is polished and evocative, though some narrative elements could be further developed to strengthen the overall impact and avoid the somewhat predictable resolution.

## Suggestions for Improvement:

* **Deepen the water rights conflict subplot**: This professional dimension could provide more complexity to their relationship and raise the stakes beyond the personal realm, making their final decision more nuanced.

* **Provide more physical description of characters**: While their emotional and psychological states are well-rendered, more vivid physical descriptions would enhance visual imagery and reader immersion.

* **Consider a less expected resolution**: The ending, while poignant, follows a somewhat predictable trajectory; exploring alternative possibilities or adding more resistance before acceptance would create greater dramatic tension.

* **Develop Brandon's character and motivations**: His role as a rival and political opportunist could be more fully explored to strengthen the antagonistic forces in the story.

* **Add more specific cultural details**: While the cultural divide is central to the story, more specific details about Northern Cheyenne traditions and values would enrich the narrative and deepen the sense of Seth's connection to his heritage.
